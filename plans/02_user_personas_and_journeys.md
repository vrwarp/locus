# User Personas & Journeys

## The Segregated Workflows

Locus serves distinct users who should never cross paths within the application interface. The UX must support strictly segregated journeys based on their roles.

### 1. The Data Custodians (Pillar 1)

#### Sarah (Church Administrator)
- **Role:** Database Admin
- **Goal:** Manage the overall health of the database, delegate tasks, and ensure compliance.
- **Journey:** Logs into the Data Custodian Workspace. Reviews the overall Entropy Rate. Assigns specific "Ghost Protocol" or Duplicate merging tasks to volunteers via the Bounty Board. Reviews and approves major data merges.

#### Emily (Volunteer)
- **Role:** Data Entry/Review Volunteer
- **Goal:** Complete assigned data cleanup tasks accurately and quickly.
- **Journey:** Logs in and is immediately placed into the Data Custodian Workspace. She engages with Gamified elements (Campus Cup, streaks) to stay motivated while churning through "Smart Fix" modals or verifying "Diagonal of Truth" anomalies. Her entire experience is task-oriented and gamified.

### 2. The Strategist (Pillar 2)

#### Dr. Robert (Executive Pastor)
- **Role:** High-Level Decision Maker
- **Goal:** Understand the health of the congregation, predict risks, and allocate pastoral resources.
- **Journey:** Logs into the Executive Dashboard. He is presented with a clean, serious, boardroom-ready interface. He views the Burnout Risk of key volunteers and the Predictive Attrition (Drift Report) of members. He does not see gamification, confetti, or any data entry tools. His workflow is entirely analytical and read-only.
