# Locus Vision & Strategy

## The Two Pillars

Locus has evolved from a general "Data Health OS" and "Ministry Intelligence Platform" into a strictly defined, two-pillar platform. We are ruthlessly focused on solving database health issues and surfacing actionable, high-level ministry strategy.

We are actively avoiding the "feature factory" syndrome. We do not build operational bloat, facilities management tools, or media suites.

### Pillar 1: Locus Core (Data Custodian Workspace)
**Target Audience:** Admins (Sarah) & Volunteers (Emily)

This is the tactical engine. It is focused entirely on reducing the "Entropy Rate" of the database.
- **Goal:** Cleanse and maintain data integrity.
- **Key Features:** Diagonal of Truth (Age/Grade anomalies), Smart Fix Modal, Ghost Protocol, Duplicates Report, Family Logic Swap.
- **UX Constraint:** This is an interactive workspace. Gamification (streaks, bounties, Campus Cup) is strictly confined *only* to this pillar to motivate repetitive data entry.

### Pillar 2: Locus Intelligence (Executive Dashboard)
**Target Audience:** Executive Pastors (Dr. Robert)

This is the predictive, high-level ROI dashboard.
- **Goal:** Surface ministry strategy from clean data.
- **Key Features:** Predictive Attrition (Drift Report), Burnout Risk, Bus Factor Graph, Retention Funnel, Check-In Velocity, Giving Trends.
- **UX Constraint:** This is a read-only, boardroom-ready dashboard. Absolutely zero gamification, confetti, or "video game" UI elements are permitted here.

## Core Principle: What We Say No To
We do not build tools outside of these two pillars. Any idea that does not directly contribute to reducing database entropy or predicting ministry health is rejected.
