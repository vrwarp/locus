## 2. Narrative Arc 1: The Skeptic's Conversion
*A story of how Dr. Robert went from budget-cutter to Locus champion.*

**Scene:** The Monday Morning Staff Meeting. Tension is high.
**Conflict:** Dr. Robert is reviewing the software budget. "We spend $5,000 a month on SaaS. We need to cut."

**The Action:**
1.  **The Challenge:** Robert points to the line item for "Locus - $99/mo." He asks, "What is this? Doesn't Planning Center do this?"
2.  **The Defense:** Sarah doesn't argue. She connects her laptop to the projector.
3.  **The Demo:** She opens Locus.
    *   She clicks "Projector Mode" (High contrast, large text).
    *   She navigates to the "Groups vs. Attendance" Scatter Plot.
4.  **The Reveal:** "Robert, look at this cluster here." She circles a group of red dots in the top right quadrant.
    *   *Data Point:* Active Online, Zero Physical Attendance (Last 6 months).
5.  **The Insight:** "These are 15 families who are effectively 'digital ghosts'. They open emails, but they haven't checked in since Easter. We are about to lose them."
6.  **The Realization:** Robert adjusts his glasses. "That's... 15 families at risk of disconnecting."
7.  **The Resolution:** Robert looks at Sarah. "Can we export that list? I want to call them personally."
8.  **The Aftermath:** Robert approves the budget. He now has Locus bookmarked on his iPad.

## 6. Narrative Arc 5: The Safe Experiment (Realized)
*How the "Sandbox" feature saved Sarah from a nervous breakdown.*

**Context:** The Youth Pastor wants to "promote" all 8th graders to 9th grade *early* for a special summer event. This affects 150 students.

**The Fear:** Sarah knows that if she messes this up, check-in will be broken for 3 months. In PCO, bulk actions are terrifyingly permanent.

**The Action:**
1.  **The Simulation:** Sarah opens Locus and toggles **"Sandbox Mode"**.
    *   *Visual:* A bright orange banner appears: **"SANDBOX MODE ACTIVE"**. The header turns striped.
2.  **The Experiment:** She runs a query: `Grade == 8`.
    *   She applies a bulk action: `Set Grade = 9`.
3.  **The Preview:** Locus generates a "Future State" scatter plot.
    *   She sees the dots move.
    *   *Warning:* Locus flags 3 students. "These 3 students are only 11 years old. Are you sure they are 8th graders?"
4.  **The Catch:** "Oh! Those are the geniuses who skipped a grade. But they aren't going to the High School retreat yet."
5.  **The Adjustment:** She removes those 3 from the selection.
6.  **The Real Thing:** She toggles Sandbox Mode **OFF**.
7.  **The Commit:** She repeats the action.
    *   *Locus:* "Are you sure? Type 'CONFIRM' to proceed."
8.  **The Outcome:** 147 records updated perfectly. 3 disasters avoided. Zero panic attacks.

## 9. Narrative Arc 8: The GDPR Panic
*When compliance becomes cool.*

**Context:** A "Right to be Forgotten" request comes in from a former member who left on bad terms. They want *all* their data deleted.

**The Risk:** In PCO, deleting a person is easy. But what about their child's check-in history? The attendance stats? Deleting them might break historical reports.

**The Locus Shield:**
1.  **The Request:** Sarah selects the person in Locus and clicks "Compliance Purge".
2.  **The Analysis:** Locus scans the entire graph.
    *   *Alert:* "User has attendance history. Cannot delete. Must Anonymize."
3.  **The Action:** Sarah confirms "Anonymize".
4.  **The Magic:**
    *   Name becomes "Anonymized User 14205".
    *   Address is scrubbed.
    *   Photo is deleted.
    *   *However:* The historical attendance data remains in the Archive, attached to the anonymous ID.
5.  **The Receipt:** Locus generates a PDF "Certificate of Erasure" for Sarah to send to the former member.
6.  **The Relief:** Legal compliance achieved. Data integrity preserved.
